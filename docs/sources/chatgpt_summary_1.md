# Public-domain / permissive post-1929 Hebrew-handwriting leads found online

## Strongest source family found
### 1) Hannah Senesh Archive — National Library of Israel
Why it matters:
- This is the clearest source family I found that contains multiple handwritten items by a deceased person.
- Hannah Senesh died in 1944.
- The archive contains diaries, manuscripts, correspondence, and related materials.
- Several specific item pages in this archive are explicitly handwritten, in Hebrew, dated after 1929, and marked "Any Use Permitted".

Collection / context URL:
- https://www.nli.org.il/en/archives/nnl_archive_al997009165988705171/NLI
- https://www.nli.org.il/en/at-your-service/announcements/hannah-szenes-archive
